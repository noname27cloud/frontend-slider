Mercury
Order from the Sun: 1
Diameter: 4,880 km
Features: Mercury is the smallest planet in the Solar System and the closest to the Sun. It has no atmosphere, leading to extreme temperature variations, ranging from -173°C at night to 427°C during the day.
Venus
Order from the Sun: 2
Diameter: 12,104 km
Features: Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is often referred to as Earth’s "sister planet" due to its similar size and composition. It has a thick atmosphere primarily composed of carbon dioxide, resulting in a high surface temperature of around 465°C, making it the hottest planet in the Solar System.
Earth
Order from the Sun: 3
Diameter: 12,742 km
Features: Earth is the only planet known to support life. It has diverse ecosystems and climates. Approximately 71% of its surface is covered by water, and its atmosphere contains oxygen, which is essential for life.
Mars
Order from the Sun: 4
Diameter: 6,779 km
Features: Mars is known as the "Red Planet" due to its iron oxide, which gives it a reddish appearance. It features the tallest volcanoes and the deepest canyons in the Solar System. Scientists are exploring the possibility of past life on Mars.
Jupiter
Order from the Sun: 5
Diameter: 139,820 km
Features: Jupiter is the largest planet in the Solar System, known for its massive storms, such as the Great Red Spot. It is a gas giant with over 79 known moons, including Ganymede, the largest moon in the Solar System.
Saturn
Order from the Sun: 6
Diameter: 116,460 km
Features: Saturn is famous for its stunning rings, composed of ice and rock particles. It is also a gas giant, with over 80 moons, including Titan, which is larger than the planet Mercury.
Uran
Order from the Sun: 7
Diameter: 50,724 km
Features: Uranus is the only planet that rotates on its side, making its axial tilt unique. It is also a gas giant with rings and over 27 known moons.
Neptun
Order from the Sun: 8
Diameter: 49,244 km
Features: Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun and is known for its strong winds, which can reach speeds of up to 2,100 km/h. It has rings and over 14 known moons, including Triton, which features active geysers.
